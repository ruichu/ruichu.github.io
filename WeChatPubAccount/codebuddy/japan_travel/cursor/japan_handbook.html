Transportation

  • Airport Transfer: Haruka Express (KIX <-> Kyoto/Osaka). Consider the ICOCA & Haruka discount package if available.
  • Local Travel: ICOCA card is key! Use on JR trains, private railways, subways, and buses in Kyoto, Osaka, Nara. Recharge at station machines.
  • Walking: Essential for exploring temple districts and neighborhoods.

Proposal Location Ideas

  • Fushimi Inari Shrine: Upper trails offer more privacy amidst the stunning red gates. Best light in morning/late afternoon.
  • Gion Shirakawa Area: Evening/dusk along the canal. Very romantic, traditional atmosphere. Check cherry blossom status if early April.
  • Arashiyama Bamboo Grove: Requires *very* early start for quiet/privacy. Magical but potentially crowded otherwise.
  • Philosopher's Path (Kyoto): If cherry blossoms are out (check forecast), a quiet morning walk here is lovely.

Map Link to preferred spot: Fushimi Inari Yotsutsuji Viewpoint (approx)

Cultural Experiences

  • Tea Ceremony: Many options in Gion/Higashiyama (e.g., Camellia, En). Book ahead. Experience matcha preparation and traditional sweets.
  • Zen Meditation (Zazen): Some temples offer English intros (e.g., Shunko-in). Check schedules and booking requirements. A calming experience.
  • Kendo: Harder to find casual experiences. May require booking a specialized tour or checking with Kyoto Budo Center for observation possibilities.

Food Guide

  • Must Try: Ramen, Sushi/Sashimi, Tempura, Okonomiyaki (Osaka specialty), Takoyaki (Osaka specialty), Udon/Soba noodles, Matcha-flavored treats, Yakitori (grilled skewers).
  • Budget Tips:
    • Utilize Konbini (convenience stores like 7-Eleven, Family Mart, Lawson) for cheap/good breakfast (onigiri, sandwiches), snacks, drinks.
    • Look for small, local ramen or noodle shops. Often have ticket machines outside.
    • Department store basements (Depachika) often have amazing food halls with samples and prepared meals, especially good value near closing time.
    • Drink tap water - it's safe.

Travel Tips

  • Money: Japan is still quite cash-based, especially at smaller shops/temples. Withdraw Yen at ATMs (7-Eleven, Post Office ATMs are reliable for foreign cards). Cards accepted at hotels, large stores, stations.
  • Connectivity: Rent pocket Wi-Fi or get a travel SIM card (pre-order or buy at airport). Free Wi-Fi can be spotty.
  • Etiquette: Bowing is common. Be quiet on trains. Don't tip. Use designated smoking areas. Remove shoes when entering homes, temples, some restaurants.
  • Trash Cans: Can be hard to find. Be prepared to carry your trash until you find one (often near vending machines or konbini).
  • Transportation: Trains are punctual. Use Google Maps or Japan Transit Planner (Jorudan/Navitime apps) for routes. Stand to the left on escalators in Kansai (right in Tokyo).
  • Power: Japan uses Type A plugs (like North America), 100V. Your US devices should work fine, but check voltage ratings.
  • Emergency: Police 110, Fire/Ambulance 119. Keep embassy/consulate info handy.
